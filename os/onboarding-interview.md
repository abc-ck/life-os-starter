# onboarding interview

**Bottom line:** Adaptive interview contract for learning the owner before personalizing the vault. It protects against copying someone else's life structure or building an elaborate system from assumptions.

**When to read this:** Read when conducting first setup or an owner-approved identity/workflow redesign.

## interview behavior

- Ask one section at a time, never the entire questionnaire at once.
- Let the owner answer conversationally by typing or voice transcript.
- Ask focused follow-ups where nuance changes the system.
- Allow the owner to skip sensitive questions.
- Separate facts, preferences, hypotheses, and unresolved choices.
- Do not edit files during discovery.
- Do not assume common folders, routines, productivity methods, or life categories apply.
- Prefer the smallest structure that can support the owner's next month.
- At the end, present a blueprint and wait for explicit approval.

## section 1 — desired outcome

Learn:

- What should this system make easier?
- What currently gets forgotten, scattered, repeated, or avoided?
- What would make the first month feel successful?
- What should this system never become?

## section 2 — real life landscape

Learn:

- roles and responsibilities
- active work and personal projects
- recurring responsibilities with no finish line
- commitments, deadlines, and people that matter
- ideas worth remembering but not activating

Do not turn every interest into a project.

## section 3 — brain and working style

Learn:

- how the owner starts, sustains, and stops work
- attention, memory, energy, and overwhelm patterns
- preferred amount of structure
- common failure or restart patterns
- what has helped or failed before
- accessibility or neurodivergence needs the owner wants included

## section 4 — tools and capture

Learn:

- devices used
- current apps and note locations
- typed, voice, email, screenshot, or mobile capture habits
- whether Obsidian is local, synced, or both
- whether old notes should be imported now or later
- which tools should remain outside the vault

## section 5 — agent relationship

Learn:

- preferred tone and response length
- how candidly the agent should challenge assumptions
- when to ask versus decide
- how many priorities/options feel manageable
- what the agent should proactively notice
- behavior that would feel irritating, controlling, vague, or unsafe

## section 6 — permissions and privacy

Learn:

- actions that always require approval
- delete/archive policy
- cloud and Git comfort
- sensitive subjects that should stay local or load only when needed
- files the agent must not rewrite without permission
- legal, medical, financial, security, communication, or relationship boundaries

Never ask for passwords, API keys, seed phrases, private keys, or authentication secrets.

## section 7 — knowledge and retrieval

Learn:

- information the owner repeatedly needs
- current sources of truth
- places where old information commonly causes mistakes
- important people/topic context
- project or business files that deserve their own local hierarchy
- current facts that must be externally verified

## section 8 — operating rhythms

Explore only routines that fit real behavior:

- startup/orientation
- daily brief
- inbox triage
- end-of-day wrap
- weekly review
- research synthesis
- project handoff
- metadata maintenance

Do not activate every supplied skill automatically.

## section 9 — versioning and backup

Learn:

- whether the owner wants local Git
- whether a private remote is acceptable
- what must be excluded
- whether any nested project/business repos should remain independent
- backup method and recovery comfort

## section 10 — blueprint approval

Present:

1. Owner profile summary.
2. Collaboration and permission rules.
3. Proposed folders, including folders deliberately not created.
4. Current projects, areas, and parked ideas.
5. Source hierarchy and retrieval routes.
6. Skills to activate now versus leave draft/remove.
7. Capture and review flow.
8. Privacy, Git, and backup plan.
9. Unknowns requiring later use rather than more interviewing.
10. Exact files/folders that will change.

Ask for corrections and explicit approval. Do not build before approval.
